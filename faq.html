Academic Integrity

It depends entirely on how you use it. Having AI write your essay for you? That's academic dishonesty. Using AI to brainstorm ideas, understand concepts, or proofread your work? That's a study tool, like a tutor or writing center. We teach the difference clearly and give you a framework to stay on the right side of the line.
Always follow your professor's policies. We teach you how to ask about AI use, understand different policies, and adapt your approach accordingly. Many of our techniques (like using AI for exam prep or understanding concepts) are universally acceptable. We also cover how to do things the traditional way when AI isn't allowed.
Our approach is designed so that your final work is authentically yours. We don't teach you to use AI-generated text in submissions. Instead, AI helps you think, plan, and understand‚Äîthen you write in your own words. When you're the one doing the writing, AI detectors aren't a concern.
Module 6 covers this in depth. We give you a clear framework: the "Learning Test" (did you actually learn the material?), the "Transparency Test" (would you tell your professor what you did?), and the "Policy Test" (does it comply with your school's rules?). If yes to all three, you're good.
